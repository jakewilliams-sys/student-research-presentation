# Competitive Perception Map

*Technical Reference — supporting analysis for internal use. For stakeholder-ready competitive findings, see the Plus Strategy Report.*

## Platform Perception Summary

- **Deliveroo** (328 mentions): Perceived as the quality/restaurant platform with the strongest student offer. Students associate Deliveroo with better restaurant selection and the student Silver tier, though service fees and minimum order thresholds cause friction.
- **Uber Eats** (309 mentions): Perceived as the ecosystem platform — transport integration (taxi, train, flights) makes Uber One feel like better value even when food prices are similar. McDonald's and late-night ordering are strongly associated with Uber Eats.
- **Just Eat** (92 mentions): Perceived as the cheaper, no-frills option for individual items. Lower brand engagement among students but preferred for specific local takeaways.
- **Other** (45 mentions): Includes Ocado, grocery delivery services, and physical restaurant visits. Grocery delivery is a separate consideration from food delivery in students' minds.

## How Students See Food Delivery Options

### Deliveroo (328 mentions)

> "I have like Deliveroo Silver. I think I don't actually entirely know what that gets me. I do have it and I do use it every now and then, but it would mostly be if it was a restaurant that isn't on Uber. I would then check Deliveroo." -- P002

> "So, I did use Just Eat a lot because I quite like how the Apache ones... But recently, I was told about the student thing where you basically get delivery service, yeah, for free as a student. So, I've been using Deliveroo a lot more..." -- P028

> "Definitely a five. I never had anything turn up after the, like, latest estimated time... Always on time or even earlier!" -- P006


### Uber Eats (309 mentions)

> "The reason that I now use Uber for essentially everything is because they have now integrated with like you can buy your plane tickets, you can buy train tickets, you can buy Eurostar tickets... And you can hire cars and stuff as well as order a taxi or order food. So because I have Uber One, you then get credits back and you can link your BA account and you can get Avios on it as well." -- P002

> "I think the biggest attraction point of Uber One is it has so many perks. Like, you get all your delivery fee and service fee taken away, but you also have those, like, 40% off on a lot of restaurants that I tend to order from. One of the other things that really attracted me is they give credits or cash back on any transport... when you book a train on Uber, you get a certain amount back and you get more as an Uber One member." -- P020

> "I will probably use it less, especially because there are a lot of places... So, like, Uber Eats didn't really work at all in my hometown. It's quite a small area in the countryside, so it doesn't get much coverage..." -- P028


### Just Eat (92 mentions)

> "Tends to be on the cheaper side. I don't know why that is. I think in my experience, they just tend to have food places that are cheaper. So I probably go with like, probably like a 4 on that one." -- P003

> "There's been one or two recently where it's like... it was, if you spend like 50, 25 quid, you get 15 quid off. Mm... that was a burger and sauce." -- P028


### Other (45 mentions)

> "I sort of bounce between all three depending on what promotions and offers are on. So I don't think I see myself ever getting a subscription for any of them just because I think I get good enough deals from just using all three, if that makes sense." -- P007

> "So, I've been doing a lot more grocery delivery lately. I happen to, like, get an Achilles tendon injury, so I don't want to, like, physically, like, take the bus and try to go to the city center and carry bags back..." -- P034

> "I mean, I guess for me there are like three options. There's like physically going into shops, there's ordering off of like Ocado or one of the shops' online delivery services, or one of these apps..." -- P034
