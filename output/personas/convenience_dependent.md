## Key Characteristics

- High frequency ordering (multiple times per week)
- Delivery integrated into daily routine as necessity
- Time constraints and busy schedules drive dependence
- Subscription services to optimize frequent usage

## Typical Weekly Pattern

Orders every few days to more than once a week, with a modal pattern of every few days. Delivery is woven into daily life—when not at home, ordering to a friend's house is common. Coordination with flatmates around lecture and gym schedules shapes meal timing. Spending on takeaways can reach £90–100+ per week despite stated money-consciousness.

## Delivery Triggers

- Daily convenience needs
- Time pressure from work/study schedules
- Lack of transportation or mobility constraints
- Weather and environmental factors

**Emotional drivers:** Stress relief and time-saving, Comfort and routine maintenance, Avoiding decision fatigue, Maintaining productivity focus

## Pain Points

- High cumulative costs from frequent ordering
- Delivery address complications in student housing
- Quality inconsistency affecting routine
- Guilt about over-reliance on delivery

## What They Say vs. What They Do

*Diary-derived insights from 2 participants in this persona who completed the 7-day diary study*

P006 said he orders "5+ times per week" but diary showed only 1 delivery order (though diary was during home week, not Dublin week). His spending was dramatic: claims "90% on takeaways, 20% on groceries" despite stating he's money-conscious.

## Deliveroo Opportunities

- Predictable daily meal solutions
- Improved student accommodation delivery logistics
- Bulk subscription discounts for frequent users
- Health-conscious convenience options
