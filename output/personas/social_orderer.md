## Key Characteristics

- Delivery becomes acceptable and less guilt-inducing in group contexts
- Social influence heavily impacts ordering decisions
- Friday evenings and weekend socializing drive orders
- Collaborative price comparison and platform choice

## Typical Weekly Pattern

Order frequency ranges from weekly to less than every three months, with a modal pattern of fortnightly or monthly. Orders cluster around Friday evenings and weekends when friends gather. Group orders are common—one person often places the order from their account (especially if they have a membership) and the group splits the cost afterward.

## Delivery Triggers

- Group gatherings and social events
- Friday evening end-of-week celebrations
- Post-night-out hunger with friends
- Peer influence and social contagion

**Emotional drivers:** Social connection and shared experiences, Reduced guilt through group participation, FOMO and peer influence, Celebration and reward mentality

## Pain Points

- Coordinating group orders and payments
- Splitting costs fairly among friends
- Different dietary preferences in groups
- Minimum order thresholds for group sizes

## What They Say vs. What They Do

*Diary-derived insights from 4 participants in this persona who completed the 7-day diary study*

P011 claimed to cook 50% of meals but diary showed only 29% home-cooked. P022 claimed to order takeaway fortnightly but placed zero delivery orders during diary week. P017 cancelled her subscription despite recognising its good value because "it just encouraged me to eat way too much takeaway."

## Deliveroo Opportunities

- Group ordering features with automatic payment splitting
- Social sharing of favorite restaurants
- Group discount thresholds
- Friday evening targeted promotions
