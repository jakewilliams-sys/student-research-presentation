## Key Characteristics

- Highly systematic approach to food and delivery decisions
- Price-conscious with sophisticated comparison strategies
- Views delivery as a calculated convenience tool rather than indulgence
- Develops structured routines and optimization systems

## Typical Weekly Pattern

Orders typically every fortnight, with some participants ordering every few days and others less than every three months. When they do order, they strategically time purchases around deals, minimum order thresholds, and post-exam or end-of-week rewards. Weekend cooking or eating out is common; weekdays are more routine-driven.

## Delivery Triggers

- Time pressure from structured schedules
- Strategic use of promotions and deals
- Post-exam or end-of-week rewards
- Convenience during busy periods

**Emotional drivers:** Control and predictability, Value optimization and smart spending, Efficiency over variety, Earned rewards after achievement

## Pain Points

- Hidden fees and price inflation on apps
- Minimum order requirements for solo ordering
- Inconsistent delivery times disrupting schedules
- Subscription value calculations

## What They Say vs. What They Do

*Diary-derived insights from 6 participants in this persona who completed the 7-day diary study*

P012 claimed "I cook 50% of the time. I order food online 50% of the time" but diary showed only 1 delivery order in 7 days and 29% home-cooked meals. Also claimed to track breakfast daily but skipped breakfast 4/7 days. P010 claimed to cook "a lot" but diary shows only 57% home-cooked meals with regular use of campus food and eating out.

## Deliveroo Opportunities

- Transparent pricing with no hidden fees
- Predictable delivery time guarantees
- Student-specific bulk ordering discounts
- Loyalty points convertible to future orders
