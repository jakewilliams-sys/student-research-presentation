*Careful financial management meets occasional treats*

## Key Characteristics

- Strict budget consciousness with delivery as calculated treat
- Low frequency ordering (monthly or less)
- Strong preference for cooking and meal prep
- Delivery reserved for special occasions or emergencies

## Typical Weekly Pattern

Orders monthly or less than every three months, with a modal pattern of monthly. Delivery is a deliberate, occasional choice—often justified by time since last order, a specific craving, or alignment with a grocery shop cycle. Breakfast routines are aspirational; diary data shows skipped meals despite stated intentions to track or eat breakfast daily.

## Delivery Triggers

- Special occasions and celebrations
- Emergency situations when cooking isn't possible
- Exceptional deals and promotions
- Social pressure in group situations

**Emotional drivers:** Financial responsibility and control, Guilt mitigation through rare usage, Value-seeking and deal optimization, Health consciousness and cooking pride

## Pain Points

- High delivery costs relative to home cooking
- Guilt about spending on 'unnecessary' convenience
- Limited budget flexibility for spontaneous orders
- Pressure to justify delivery expenses

## What They Say vs. What They Do

*Diary-derived insights from 2 participants in this persona who completed the 7-day diary study*

P004 is the "newly breakfast convert" who claimed to track breakfast daily but diary showed she skipped breakfast 3/7 days due to time constraints. P005 claimed to order delivery "maybe twice a month" but ordered twice in one week during diary study.

## Deliveroo Opportunities

- Student-specific pricing tiers
- Emergency meal solutions at lower cost
- Cooking ingredient delivery for meal prep
- Occasional treat positioning rather than routine convenience
