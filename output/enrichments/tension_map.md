# Student Food Delivery Tensions

*110 tensions identified across 33 of 35 participants (2 showed no coded tensions)*

*Some quotes appear across multiple categories where the participant's words evidence more than one tension.*

## Core Paradoxes Students Live With

### 1. Health vs Convenience (22 tensions, 9 participants)

- *Professional dietitian identity conflicts with actual eating behavior*
  > "I'm definitely more of eating out person or, you know, getting takeaway person that I am a cooking person. Although I'm trying to change that this year." -- P017

- *Professional knowledge vs personal behavior - dietitian who orders takeaway frequently*
  > "I actually am a dietitian as well. So yeah, I, well, I tried to follow the, you know, dietitians or the least misinformationy people out there." -- P017

- *Professional knowledge ('I should be eating healthy') vs practical constraints*
  > "It's just because I don't have any time to prepare it. So, like, you know, you're kind of like torn between, like, I'm actually starving right now and I'm like sat here on a lecture, you know, and then, yeah, like, I should be eating healthy. Like, you know, I should kind of, you know, prepare something and whatever. But then you're kind of like, well, it's actually going to take me half an hour to prepare that, and then it's going to take half an hour to cook. And, you know, it's like everything is just time, and half an hour is probably even like under an underestimate of the time to prepare. It's like, you know, everything takes. Takes time. And also, having the correct ingredients first requires a bit of preparation in advance. That you have to have the right ingredients and all of that kind of stuff." -- P017

- *contradicts later statements about not having time to cook*
  > "I really like cooking, so I do watch a lot of Gordon Ramsay myself. I do follow food influencers, restaurants as well, and all of that." -- P006

- *contrasts with earlier statement about loving cooking*
  > "Oh, maybe twice a week... Definitely twice a week. I wouldn't say more." -- P006

### 3. Aspiration vs Reality (18 tensions, 14 participants)

*Students' self-image as healthy, organised eaters conflicts with their actual behaviour*

- *Mental health and academic pressure undermine eating intentions*
  > "Yeah, that's more tied to things like mental health and, like, if I'm failing exams... stuff like that." -- P028

- *Meal-planning aspirations don't survive the day*
  > "Lunch... I try to bring some food with me if I've made anything, like meal-planned, but I'm not always super great about that. And then if I don't, then I just, like, try to go to one of the restaurants around." -- P034

- *Positive delivery associations override plan to cook*
  > "Delivery? Yeah. Still like my associations... It's better for food delivery, and I've had good experiences with them in general. Like, no one's stolen my food off of it! And this thing that you told me about... the free delivery service." -- P034

- *Diary shows only 1 delivery order in 7 days, contradicting 50/50 claim*

- *Shows frequent breakfast skipping pattern*

### 4. Say-Do Gap (14 tensions, 9 participants)

- *Claims to 'really like food and eating' but describes purely utilitarian relationship*
  > "I know what I like and I like what I know with cooking. In an ideal world, I would experiment a little bit more, but at the minute, I don't really have the time. And it's not something that's too important to me. Like, I really like food and I really like eating, but I'm someone that can, like, eat the same things over and over again and, like, not get bored of it." -- P003

- *Claims to track breakfast daily but diary shows frequent skipping*
  > "I'd say lunch is more social for me because I have it with my friends here at the university. And for functional, say I have my breakfast. Like I try to track my breakfast every day, like every nutrients in my breakfast, like the protein, the carbs, the everything." -- P012

- *says Deliveroo but means the cheaper option (which would be Uber in this scenario)*
  > "I'd probably go for Deliveroo... so the cheaper one, even though it's the euro. But still, I'd like to save the euro, maybe put it towards the tip. So, yeah." -- P006

- *Interview claims 1-2 orders per week but diary shows more restrained behavior*
  > "Then for lunch, after I got in from my lecture, which is a long four hour lecture, I was really tired. And there wasn't much food in the cupboard, and I couldn't really, be bothered to go to the shop and get something to eat. So I pardon me, used a I mean, used Uber Eats to order a takeaway. So I got, some chicken and rice from a noodle bar, and I don't normally order this early into the day." -- P015

- *Claims to be 'newly breakfast convert' but diary shows skipped breakfast 3/7 days*

### 5. Routine Disruption (13 tensions, 10 participants)

*Academic schedules, social events, and mood swings override established food routines*

- *Busy day causes complete meal skipping despite normal lunch routine*
  > "So today past couple of days actually been pretty busy. But today, particularly, I was at uni in the morning, then I had a doctor's appointment across the city, and then I had to go back to uni for a meeting in the afternoon, and then literally got home at, like, yeah, like, half four, quarter to five or something. So I'm not usually a breakfast person anyway, but I definitely didn't have breakfast today. Normally, when I've been at uni, I would have had lunch, but because I had, like, doctor's appointments and thing and I was, like, across the city, I didn't have lunch today." -- P002

- *Early lectures repeatedly override established breakfast routine*
  > "So today in the morning, I skipped my breakfast because I had my lectures early. Then in the afternoon, I just bought something from Tesco, like, a meal leaf from Tesco. Had it. It was, like, a protein shake, a wrap, a chicken tikka wrap, and, protein fudge, I guess, for snacks." -- P012

- *Weekend behaviour contradicts weekday discipline*
  > "Weekends, I go out by myself, and I go to the place physically. It's like if I'm going for a takeaway or a restaurant, I go in myself and with the social group." -- P006

- *Usually orders when alone but didn't this time - shows variability in solo ordering*
  > "So I didn't get, I didn't get one this evening, even though I was on my own, which I would probably usually do. But, yeah, I wasn't in the mood to cook, too much. And, yeah, this is fairly reflective of a normal kind of evening day for me." -- P017

- *contrasts weekday delivery dependency with weekend social dining*
  > "Weekends, I go out by myself, and I go to the place physically. It's like if I'm going for a takeaway or a restaurant, I go in myself and with the social group." -- P006

- *contrasts weekday delivery dependency with weekend social preference*
  > "So, during weekdays, Monday to Friday, and like weekends, maybe once... but I try to avoid it on weekends because I like going out more on weekends when I have time." -- P006

- *Strong social eating preference contradicts individual food decision-making*

But our flat is also very social, so anytime I go in the kitchen, it's almost guaranteed I'm gonna have someone to eat with. That's just my main priority, no matter who it is..." -- P036
