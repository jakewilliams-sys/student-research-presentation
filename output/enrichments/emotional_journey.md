# Emotional Journey Map (Pilot — N=2)

## P001 (Lee) — The Treat Seeker Journey

### Pre-Order
- **Emotion**: Anticipation + mild guilt
- **Trigger**: Friday evening, end of a long week, social context
- **Inner dialogue**: "I deserve this... but I shouldn't be having it"
- **Platform consideration**: Defaults to familiar local takeaway; doesn't consider aggregators

### Decision Point
- **Emotion**: Hesitation
- **Key factor**: Social context determines outcome — with friends, proceeds easily; alone, guilt creates friction
- **Platform role**: Minimal — prefers calling restaurant directly for trust/dispute reasons

### Ordering
- **Emotion**: Commitment anxiety
- **Pain point**: Fee stacking (delivery + service + surcharges) creates regret before food arrives
- **Desired state**: Clear all-in pricing so the treat feels uncomplicated

### Waiting
- **Emotion**: Anticipation (if social) / slight anxiety (if solo)
- **Context**: Past incident of neighbour taking delivery creates ambient distrust

### Consumption
- **Emotion**: Enjoyment (in the moment)
- **Social context amplifies**: "Less guilty and more like a social thing" with friends

### Post-Order
- **Emotion**: Mild regret about health/spending (solo) or satisfaction (social)
- **Reorder trigger**: Next Friday ritual

## P009 (Aayush) — The Price Optimizer Journey

### Pre-Order
- **Emotion**: Pragmatic / time-pressured
- **Trigger**: Academic schedule pressure, tiredness, group occasion
- **Inner dialogue**: "I need food, let me find the best deal"

### Decision Point
- **Emotion**: Analytical engagement (almost competitive)
- **Key factor**: Opens Deliveroo, Uber Eats, AND Just Eat to compare prices on the same items
- **Platform role**: Central — but interchangeable; no emotional attachment to any platform

### Ordering
- **Emotion**: Satisfaction when deal is found; mild frustration at price inconsistencies
- **Pain point**: Same item priced differently across platforms erodes trust
- **Desired state**: Price match guarantee that eliminates the need to comparison shop

### Waiting
- **Emotion**: Neutral — ordering is routine, not an event

### Consumption
- **Emotion**: Not discussed — food is functional, not experiential (notable absence)
- **Social context**: Group orders create an organiser role that Aayush enjoys

### Post-Order
- **Emotion**: Cashback satisfaction (Uber One 5% credit)
- **Reorder trigger**: Next meal need (2-3 days)
