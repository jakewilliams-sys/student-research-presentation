# Emotional Journey Through Ordering

*Technical Reference — supporting analysis for internal use. For stakeholder-ready findings, see the Research Report and Topline Summary.*

## Narrative Interpretation

The dominant emotion across all ordering stages is **indifference** (521 of 1,344 tagged segments, 39%). This does not mean students don't care about food delivery — rather, it reflects the **low emotional salience of routine food decisions**. Most meal-related segments describe habitual behaviour ("I just grabbed something quick") that is emotionally flat by nature.

When indifference is filtered out as a noise emotion (consistent with the journey map and micro-moment analyses), the emotional landscape shifts significantly:
- **Trigger stage:** stress (95) and comfort (75) dominate — ordering is prompted by either pressure or a desire for ease
- **Social context:** connection (63) and comfort (55) lead — group ordering is emotionally positive
- **Engagement stage:** frustration (65) and excitement (57) compete — the app experience generates both friction and anticipation
- **General:** stress (59) and comfort (42) — the broader food environment is a source of tension and relief

**Key implication for Deliveroo:** The emotional opportunity is not in the ordering moment itself (which is largely emotionally neutral) but in the **trigger** (positioning delivery as stress relief) and **social context** (amplifying the connection and comfort of shared ordering). Marketing that tries to make ordering feel exciting may miss the mark — students want delivery to feel easy and socially natural.
