Student Food & Delivery Research

What do you think you know?

Before we share what we found, let's test some assumptions about how students eat, order, and choose platforms.

35 Interviews 6 In-home visits 15 Diary participants 315 Meal occasions

How we got here

35 participants (UG, PG, graduate segments), 315 meal occasions (seven days), 6 in-home visits

We're going to show you five statements about students.

For each one, discuss whether you think it's true.

01

Students cook consistently throughout the week.

It depends on the day

Home cooking drops from 71% on weekdays to 43% on weekends — a 29-point collapse.

Students do cook most of their meals during the week. But from Friday onwards, routines break down. Social plans override cooking intentions, fridges empty out, and tiredness from the week takes over. The weekend is a completely different food landscape.

Diary study, n=15 · 315 meal occasions tracked

02

Food delivery is a regular part of student life.

Not what we found

Delivery represents just 3.5% of actual meals eaten — despite students thinking about it constantly.

Students do cook most of their meals during the week. But from Friday onwards, routines break down. Social plans override cooking intentions, fridges empty out, and tiredness from the week takes over. The weekend is a completely different food landscape.

Diary study, n=15 · 315 meal occasions tracked

03

Students order delivery for themselves, as a personal treat.

Not the primary driver

Social context is the strongest behavioural driver. Group ordering drives both frequency and platform choice.

Students do cook most of their meals during the week. But from Friday onwards, routines break down. Social plans override cooking intentions, fridges empty out, and tiredness from the week takes over. The weekend is a completely different food landscape.

31 quotes from 22 participants · High confidence

04

Students are loyal to one delivery platform.

Not what we found

Students are radically platform-agnostic. Price comparison is a group sport — three apps open, fees compared side by side.

Students do cook most of their meals during the week. But from Friday onwards, routines break down. Social plans override cooking intentions, fridges empty out, and tiredness from the week takes over. The weekend is a completely different food landscape.

16 quotes from 20 participants

05

Students who subscribe to delivery platforms use them regularly.

Not what we found

Many students hold subscriptions they barely use — kept 'just in case' or forgotten entirely.

Students sign up during promotional periods or when a friend shares an offer, then keep the subscription running out of inertia. Some can't remember which platforms they're subscribed to. Others justify the cost by saying they'll 'use it more next month.' The subscription doesn't drive regular ordering — it just sits there, a low-level background cost that rarely converts into habitual behaviour.

Subscription discussed by 28 of 35 participants · Low active usage relative to sign-up rate
