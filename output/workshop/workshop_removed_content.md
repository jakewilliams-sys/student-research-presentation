## Detailed Uber One Competitive Analysis

Uber One's cross-service ecosystem creates a current competitive advantage over Deliveroo Plus that food delivery benefits alone cannot overcome. 7 participants explicitly value Uber One for taxi/train integration, making the subscription 'worth it' even during periods of low food ordering. Uber One is referenced by 24 participants (14 current subscribers, 10 non-subscribers who mentioned it) versus 11 current Deliveroo Plus subscribers, and 9 students hold both subscriptions — defaulting to Uber Eats for routine orders while using Deliveroo when specific restaurants or deals make it worthwhile.

Deliveroo is competing against an ecosystem, not just a food delivery subscription. Uber One subscribers see food delivery as a bonus on top of transport value, creating stickiness that makes them unlikely to switch even when Deliveroo offers better individual deals.

### Subscription Sentiment (for discussion)

Among undergraduates: 65 negative sentiment segments vs 38 positive. Among postgraduates: 13 negative vs 7 positive. Negative sentiment coexists with continued usage because per-order savings create genuine utility — the subscription "works" economically even when students feel negatively about it.

## Pause/Resume Finding

**Subscription pause/resume is an unmet student need that drives unnecessary churn.** Students have 3-4 predictable non-ordering periods per year (Christmas break, Easter, summer holiday, study abroad) during which they either keep paying for unused subscriptions or cancel entirely.

> "You can pause it anytime... say you're going home for Christmas, pausing membership." — P025

**Note for workshop discussion:** Students currently get Plus Silver free with their university email. Pause/resume may be more relevant for paid Plus subscribers or as a future consideration if the free model changes. Explore whether this applies to the student proposition or is better positioned for non-student segments.

### Pause/Resume Recommendations

- [Product] Introduce a 'Pause Plus' feature allowing students to pause their subscription for 1-4 weeks during holiday periods, with automatic resumption at term start.
- [Product] Proactively prompt students to pause (not cancel) when the app detects reduced ordering frequency or when academic calendars indicate break periods.
- [Marketing] Position the pause feature as proof that Deliveroo understands student life: 'We know you're home for Christmas — your Plus is paused and waiting for you.'

## Three-Tier Plus Recommendation

**[Product] Develop three stage-specific Plus tiers:** student tier (free delivery focus), postgrad tier (health-forward restaurant curation + social features), graduate tier (premium restaurant access + quality positioning).

Each stage has fundamentally different value drivers that a single subscription model cannot address. Undergraduates need price-first messaging with emotional 'treat' positioning; postgraduates respond to health-conscious, convenience-first, socially-aware messaging; graduates are most receptive to premium emotional positioning.

**Workshop questions:**
- How does this interact with the current free Silver model?
- Is tiering feasible within the existing subscription architecture?
- What does the postgrad tier look like given the small sample (n=4)?

## CRM Transition Detection Recommendation

**[Retention] Build transition detection into CRM to identify stage shifts (graduation, postgrad enrollment) and proactively adjust value proposition before churn occurs.**

The dramatic shift in drivers between stages (especially the emotional drop for postgraduates) creates high churn risk at transition points. Graduation is the highest churn-risk moment — graduates show the lowest subscription engagement.

**Workshop questions:**
- What data signals can we use to detect stage transitions?
- How does this connect to the existing student lifecycle in CRM?

## Graduation Bridge Recommendation

**[Retention] Create a 'graduation bridge' offer that transitions graduating students to paid Plus with an introductory rate before they default to Uber One in post-university life.**

Graduates show the lowest subscription engagement. Graduation is when free Silver ends, creating a natural churn point. Without a bridge offer, students default to whatever platform they're already using — often Uber One due to transport value.

## Academic Stage Detailed Findings

Three distinct behavioural patterns by academic stage (for workshop deep dive):

**Undergraduates** — High price sensitivity (12.9 mentions/person) combined with moderate emotional reward framing (2.4/person). Price is the dominant lens, but the Friday treat ritual provides emotional permission to spend.

**Postgraduates** (n=4, directional only) — Uniquely functional group. Least emotionally driven (0.2 mentions/person), most health-aware (100%), least cooking-oriented (3.2/person). Time scarcity and professional identity create a different relationship with convenience.

**Graduates** — Unexpectedly show the highest emotional reward framing (3.0/person, 100%) alongside the lowest price sensitivity (7.3/person). They can afford delivery and no longer feel guilty — delivery becomes a genuine lifestyle choice rather than a budget trade-off.
