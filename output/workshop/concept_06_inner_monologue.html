Inner Monologue

Follow Their Order — Four students. Six stages. One journey.

Same app. Four completely different experiences. This is the journey told as each persona's internal voice — the audience inside the student's head.

The Pragmatic Optimizer

Systematic efficiency meets strategic deal-hunting

"Ordering a takeaway after an exam… that's an enjoyable meal. It's like a reward. You've earned it."— P007
Deciding to order
Comfort

Thursday evening. Lectures done, library closed. I could cook… but do I really want to? After a day like that, I've earned something better. The question isn't whether to order — it's how to order smart.

"It sort of takes you out from when you're doing, like, work all day. It's just something different that you can focus on."
— P025
Optimizer
Choosing where & what
Stress

Three apps. Three sets of delivery fees. I know if I add a drink I can hit £15 and save on delivery. It's not complicated — it's just maths. But it takes time. Time I'd rather not spend.

"I try to make it about £15 to save delivery fees… it makes some changes in my delivery order."
— P012
Optimizer
Placing the order
Excitement

Done. Order's in. The fee was manageable, the deal was good enough. Now I just wait. That feeling — the anticipation? That's the reward starting already.

Optimizer
Waiting for delivery
Neutral

Twenty-five minutes. I'll do something else. The hard part was deciding, not waiting.

Optimizer
Eating the food
Comfort

Exactly what I expected. Nothing wrong with that. A quiet reward after a calculated decision.

Optimizer
After the meal
Excitement

Worth it. The math checked out. A productive day, capped with something I earned.

"Whether that's through a productive day or I've gone on a long hike or something. Where you've earned it."

The Social Orderer

Group dynamics drive delivery decisions

"Oh, it's like an unspoken ritual… We're not meant to call it a 'ritual,' but it definitely is."— P001
Deciding to order
Stress

My mate just mentioned food. That's all it takes. Now there's a seed in my head and I can already feel Friday night happening.

"I sat next to my mate, and he was like, 'Oh, I just think I'm going to order something.' And that's all, like, planted a seed in my head."
— P015
Social
Choosing where & what
Stress

Everyone's got opinions. Different restaurants, different apps. Someone's got to take charge. Problem is, if the food's bad, that's on me.

"Something about takeaways at home even with friends don't feel as social. Whereas if you're out and about together it feels more social for some reason."
— P022
Social
Placing the order
Connection

One phone, my account, my membership. We'll split it later. For a moment everyone's aligned — this is our Friday night.

"I ordered from my account. I have a membership, so I ordered from my account all together and then we split it in like three or four."
— P009
Social
Waiting for delivery
Neutral

Someone puts on a show. The food's coming. That's all that matters right now.

Social
Eating the food
Guilt

The food's eaten. The energy's different now. Was that too much? When I'm with friends it doesn't feel as bad. But alone with my thoughts… the guilt creeps in.

"When you're going out with friends, it doesn't feel as bad getting a pizza. It feels more like a social thing rather than ordering it to your house and eating it on your own."
— P001
Social
After the meal
Comfort

Plates stacked, containers gone. The evening settles into that comfort that only comes from routine. Next Friday, same thing.

The Budget Balancer

Careful financial management meets occasional treats

"I'm not really the kind of person that likes having subscriptions anyway because I just don't… like money coming out."— P023
Deciding to order
Stress

Has it been long enough? Is there food in the fridge? Am I actually craving this or just being lazy? Three conditions. All three need a yes before I let myself order.

"Justified is probably just when it's been a while, if it's something I've been feeling like having, and if it's time to do another grocery shop."
— P005
Balancer
Choosing where & what
Comfort

Permission granted. Now the guilt lifts and it's actually… nice. Finding the right deal, the right restaurant. The hard part is over.

Balancer
Waiting for delivery

"I've had quite a busy or stressful week as, like, a little reward."

The Convenience Dependent

Delivery as essential life infrastructure

"Usually, when I'm not at home, I'm always ordering off Just Eat, Deliveroo, or UberEats, ordering to the friend's house. About pretty much five times a week."— P006
Deciding to order
Frustration

Cereal's gone. Milk's out. The shop is twenty minutes in February rain. The frustration isn't about food — it's everything else getting in the way of eating.

"I started noticing that we're running out of cereal, eggs, milk, porridge… I didn't feel like going down to the shop… and I didn't have the time."
— P006
Dependent
Choosing where & what
Comfort

The app's already open. The regular order's right there. The only real question is which platform has the better deal tonight. It's barely a decision.

"I have one main flatmate that I usually coordinate with, like when our lectures are, when we'll go to the gym, and so that we try to eat around the same time."
— P036
Dependent
Placing the order
Connection

Order placed. From a friend's house, between lectures, coordinated with my flatmate. This isn't an event. It's infrastructure. And it's working.
