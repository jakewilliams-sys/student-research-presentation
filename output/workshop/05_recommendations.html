Opportunity 1

Make "free delivery" genuinely free

Free delivery is the foundation of the Plus proposition. But service fees and minimum order thresholds are diluting the promise before students feel the value.

Critical
47
mentions of free delivery — more than every other benefit combined
27 service fee complaints 15 minimum order frustrations
  • Remove or absorb service fees for Plus subscribers so the saving is felt immediately at checkout
  • Lower or remove minimum order thresholds — solo student orders (£8–12) currently fall below the £15–25 floor
  • Lead all Plus messaging with "free delivery on every order" as the singular, unambiguous hero benefit

Experience Gaps

"To me, the best thing is that it scratches off the delivery fees. Most of the time it's above £2. So you're saving £2 every time."

— Mrugesh

Opportunity 2

Build value beyond food

Uber One's advantage isn't food — it's transport. Plus needs its own version of that anchor to make the subscription feel valuable between orders.

Critical

The ecosystem gap

Give Plus a reason to exist between delivery orders.

  • Explore strategic partnerships — entertainment, lifestyle, or campus services — that create value outside of food delivery
  • Create a visible "what you've earned" touchpoint — credits, rewards, or perks that remind students the subscription exists between orders
  • Position Plus as part of student life, not just part of ordering — the subscription should feel present even when students aren't hungry

35% of Uber One discussants cite transport as the reason they subscribe · Plus currently has no value driver outside food

Value Gap

"Uber One also comes with not just food delivery discounts, but also Uber ride discounts. So I think that's why I loved it more."

— Sian

Opportunity 3

Win the group moment

Delivery isn't an individual decision. It's a flatmate, a group chat, a Friday night negotiation. The product should meet students where the decision actually happens.

High

The social opportunity

Design for the group, not the individual.

  • Develop collaborative group ordering with shared carts, split payment, and group decision tools
  • Shift student marketing from individual convenience to social enablement — "order together" rather than "order for yourself"
  • Target Thursday–Friday social planning moments when the weekend ordering decision is being made

Social influence is the primary delivery trigger · Home cooking drops 17 points from weekday to weekend · Group dynamics drive the majority of ordering decisions

Group Decision · Weekend Collapse

"It's always a group decision. One person suggests it, and then everyone's like, yeah, let's just get a Deliveroo."

— Evan

Opportunity 4

Flex with the student calendar

The subscription is free — but attention isn't. Students disengage completely over holidays, and each new term requires re-building the habit. Graduation is the moment when real churn happens.

Medium

The engagement cycle

Keep the habit alive across the academic year.

  • Re-engagement campaigns at the start of each term — the ordering habit resets every time students return from a break
  • Freshers acquisition: the start of university is the peak receptivity window — double down on this as the primary sign-up moment
  • Build a graduation bridge — an introductory paid Plus rate that retains students as they transition to professional life, where the real churn risk lives

3–4 non-ordering windows per year · Graduation is the highest churn-risk moment · 9 freshers-related acquisition moments identified

Student Calendar · Engagement Cycle

"Depending on where I live after… whether I go back home or move to another city… it will really change based on what I'm doing with my life, whether I've got a job or whether I'm still studying."

— Evan · Graduation as inflection point

Opportunity 5

Activate Silver subscribers

Students who got Plus through Prime are already subscribers. They just don't know what they have — or why it matters.

High

The onboarding opportunity

Turn passive subscribers into active ones.

  • Create an active onboarding flow for Prime-acquired Silver subscribers — show them what they have and what it saves them
  • Build a savings dashboard that surfaces the value of their subscription after each order
  • Design a clear upgrade path from Silver to paid Plus, timed to when students are seeing the benefits

38% of those who discussed Plus/Silver can't explain what it gives them · Prime creates subscribers but not engagement

Experience Gaps · Onboarding

"I think this must be included with my Prime or something. I don't actually entirely know what that gets me."

— Rory
